What draws me to engineering is curiosity. I like asking why something behaves the way it does and then slowly figuring it out. It's this steady curiosity that guides how I work through challenges. Sketching an idea, refining it, testing it, learning from what didn’t work - there's something really satisfying about seeing a system respond the way you intended.

I really value working with other people. Some of my best projects have come from long discussions, shared problem-solving, and numerous small iterations made together.

Currently an Undergraduate Researcher at the IDEELab, working on next-generation stacked piezoelectric actuators with optimized displacement and load capacity.
